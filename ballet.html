Ballet is a type of performance dance that originated during the Italian Renaissance in the fifteenth century and later developed into
a concert dance form in France and Russia. It has since become a widespread, highly technical form of dance with its own vocabulary
based on French terminology.It has been globally influential and has defined the foundational techniques used in many other dance
genres and cultures. Ballet has been taught in various schools around the world, which have historically incorporated their own cultures
and as a result, the art has evolved in a number of distinct ways. See glossary of ballet.A ballet, a work, consists of the choreography
and music for a ballet production. Ballets are choreographed and performed by trained ballet dancers. Traditional classical ballets are
usually performed with classical music accompaniment and use elaborate costumes and staging, whereas modern ballets, such as the
neoclassical works of American choreographer George Balanchine, are often performed in simple costumes (e.g., leotards and tights)
and without the use of elaborate sets or scenery.
